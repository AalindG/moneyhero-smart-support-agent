# Personal Loan Basics FAQ

## What is a personal loan?

A personal loan is an unsecured loan that provides a lump sum of cash, repaid in fixed monthly instalments over an agreed tenure. "Unsecured" means no collateral is required — the loan is approved based on your income and creditworthiness.

## What can I use a personal loan for?

Personal loans in Singapore can be used for:
- Debt consolidation (combining multiple debts into one lower-rate loan)
- Home renovation
- Medical expenses
- Wedding expenses
- Education fees
- Emergency funds

Banks generally do not restrict usage, but the loan cannot be used for illegal activities or investments in volatile assets.

## What is the difference between flat rate and EIR?

**Flat rate**: Calculated on the original loan principal. E.g., 3.5% flat on $10,000 = $350/year in interest.

**EIR (Effective Interest Rate)**: The true annual cost, accounting for the fact that your principal reduces as you repay. EIR is always higher than the flat rate — often 2x. Always compare EIR, not flat rate.

## How much can I borrow?

MAS (Monetary Authority of Singapore) regulations cap unsecured borrowing at **12x your monthly income** in aggregate across all banks and financial institutions.

Individual bank limits:
- Typically up to 4–10x your monthly salary
- Based on your income, existing debts, and credit score

## What affects my loan approval?

Key factors:
- **Income**: Must meet minimum income threshold ($20,000–$30,000/year)
- **Credit score**: Higher score = better rates and higher approval likelihood
- **Debt-to-income ratio**: Existing loan and card commitments vs. income
- **Employment stability**: Permanent employment preferred; self-employed may need 2 years of NOA
- **Age**: Must be 21–65 years old

## What is a debt consolidation plan (DCP)?

A DCP is a special government-facilitated loan that allows you to consolidate all your unsecured debts (credit cards, personal loans) across all banks into a single loan at a lower interest rate. Eligible if your total unsecured debt exceeds 12x your monthly income.

## Can I repay my loan early?

Yes, but most banks charge an **early repayment fee** of 1%–2% of the outstanding principal, or a flat fee of ~$150–$250. Calculate whether the interest savings outweigh the penalty before deciding.

## How do I improve my chances of approval?

- Check your CBS credit report and resolve any errors before applying
- Reduce outstanding balances on existing credit cards
- Avoid applying for multiple loans simultaneously (each application is a hard inquiry)
- Apply during stable employment — avoid applications during probation periods
- Use MyInfo for faster, more accurate verification
